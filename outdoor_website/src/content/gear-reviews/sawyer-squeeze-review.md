## Bottom Line

The best all-around backpacking water filter at any price. Lightweight, reliable, fast enough, and the 0.1 micron hollow fiber filter removes bacteria and protozoa without chemicals or waiting. After 200+ miles of Colorado trails, it's still what gets recommended when someone asks.

## Specs

- Weight: 3 oz (filter only)
- Filter life: 100,000 gallons (lifetime with proper care)
- Flow rate: ~1.7 liters/minute when new
- Removes: bacteria, protozoa (does NOT remove viruses — see note below)
- Price: ~$35

## What It Does Well

**Weight.** At 3 oz it's lighter than most water bottles. Combined with a 1L Smartwater bottle (the standard carry setup), the whole filtration system weighs under 5 oz.

**Simplicity.** Fill the bag, attach the filter, squeeze into a bottle. No waiting, no tablets, no pumping. The learning curve is measured in seconds.

**Longevity.** 100,000 gallons is more than any backpacker will ever use. With proper backflushing the filter essentially lasts forever.

**Cost.** $35 for a filter that never expires is outstanding value.

## Backflushing

Flow rate degrades noticeably after 50–100 liters if you don't backflush regularly. The included syringe is for backflushing — use it every 2-3 days on trail or whenever flow starts to slow. Takes 30 seconds and restores full flow rate.

If you skip backflushing for too long, the filter can be impossible to restore. This is the #1 reason people think their Sawyer "broke."

## Viruses

The Sawyer Squeeze does NOT filter viruses. In North American backcountry this is generally not a concern — wilderness water sources rarely carry viral contamination. If you're traveling internationally or in areas with heavy human activity near water sources, add Aquatabs or use a SteriPen.

## Setup

The standard setup: carry a 1L Smartwater bottle as your dirty water source, squeeze through the Sawyer directly into another bottle or your reservoir. Many hikers also screw the Sawyer directly onto a standard Smartwater bottle for a clean, lightweight system.

## Durability

Two years of use, multiple extended trips, hundreds of miles. The filter itself shows no wear. The included squeeze bags are fragile — most people replace them with Smartwater bottles within the first trip.
