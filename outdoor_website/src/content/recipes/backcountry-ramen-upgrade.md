## Why Ramen

Instant ramen is the backbone of budget backpacking food. At $0.25/packet and 380 calories, it's hard to beat on weight-to-calorie ratio. The problem is it's not very filling and tastes like sodium. The fix is simple: add fat and protein.

## Ingredients

- 1 packet instant ramen (any flavor) — 380 cal, 1.5 oz
- 1 tbsp olive oil — 120 cal, 0.5 oz
- 1 packet shelf-stable bacon bits — 50 cal, 0.5 oz
- 1 packet parmesan or cheddar powder — 50 cal, 0.3 oz

**Total: ~600 cal, 2.8 oz**

## Instructions

1. Boil 2 cups of water
2. Add ramen noodles, cook 3 minutes
3. Drain most of the water (leave a splash)
4. Add flavor packet, olive oil, bacon bits, and cheese powder
5. Stir and eat immediately

## Variations

**Spicy version:** Add a packet of Cholula hot sauce (found in single-serve packets at most restaurants). Adds almost zero weight and transforms the flavor.

**Protein boost:** Add a tuna packet (100 cal, 2.6 oz) for a genuinely filling meal that clears 700 calories.

**Vegetable add-in:** Bring a small handful of dried mushrooms (sprinkle them in with the boiling water). They rehydrate while the ramen cooks and add texture without much weight.

## Calorie Density

At 600 cal for 2.8 oz, this is approximately 214 cal/oz — well above the 100 cal/oz benchmark most backpackers target. It's one of the most efficient dinners you can carry.

## Cost

Entire meal comes in under $2, vs $10–14 for a freeze-dried dinner of similar calories. For multi-week trips, the savings add up fast.
