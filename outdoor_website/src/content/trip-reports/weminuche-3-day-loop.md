## The Route

We started at the Vallecito Trailhead and followed the creek north before cutting east to meet the Pine River Trail on day two. The loop closes back through the wilderness boundary — about 28 miles total with 4,200 feet of cumulative gain.

## Day 1 — Vallecito Creek to Camp

The first 8 miles are deceptively easy. Flat trail along the creek, good footing, and consistent shade. We made camp just past the wilderness boundary near a wide bend in the creek. Water was abundant and clear all the way up.

**What worked:** The early miles let us settle into a rhythm before the real climbing started on day two.

**What didn't:** We packed too much food. Between three people we brought 6 pounds of food per day — way more than we needed on the easy first day.

## Day 2 — The Climb

This is where the trip earned its elevation gain. The trail cuts steeply through open meadows above treeline before dropping into the drainage. Big views of the Needle Mountains to the west if the afternoon clouds hold off (they didn't for us).

**Pro tip:** Start before 7am on day 2. Afternoon thunderstorms roll in fast above treeline in August.

## Day 3 — Pine River Back Out

The Pine River Trail is beautiful but exposed. Long stretches of open meadow with nowhere to hide if weather moves in. We hit light rain for the last 4 miles — nothing serious, but a reminder to always have your rain gear accessible, not buried.

## Food Notes

Using the Trail Provisions calorie calculator before this trip we targeted 3,200 cal/day at moderate activity. We actually hit about 2,800 on day 1 (easier than expected) and probably burned closer to 3,800 on day 2. The calculator's moderate estimate was a good middle-ground anchor.

Standout meals:
- **Breakfast:** Oatmeal with Justin's peanut butter and a packet of instant coffee — ~520 cal, done in 10 minutes
- **Dinner night 2:** Mountain House Beef Stroganoff — expensive at $13 but genuinely good after a hard day

## Gear Notes

- Osprey Atmos 65 performed well on the loaded first day. Switched to a lighter 40L for day 2's summit push.
- Sawyer Squeeze filter — no issues, used it every mile along Vallecito Creek
- Brought too many layers. One midlayer would have been enough in August.
